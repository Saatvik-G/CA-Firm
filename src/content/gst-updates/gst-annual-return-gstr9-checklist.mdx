## Statutory Applicability Thresholds

- **Form GSTR-9 (Annual Return)**: Mandatory for registered taxpayers with aggregate turnover exceeding **₹2 Crores** in the relevant financial year.
- **Form GSTR-9C (Reconciliation Statement)**: Mandatory self-certified reconciliation statement for taxpayers with aggregate turnover exceeding **₹5 Crores**.

---

## 1. Key Table-Wise Reconciliation Checklist

```
Table 4 (Outward Supplies Taxable)  ──> Match with Audited Financial P&L Turnover
Table 6 (ITC Availed during FY)     ──> Match with Purchase Ledgers & GSTR-3B total
Table 8 (ITC as per GSTR-2A/2B)     ──> Compare against Table 6 ITC claimed
Table 9 (Tax Paid vs. Payable)      ──> Verify Cash Ledger & Credit Ledger debits
```

---

## 2. Critical Audit Verification Areas

1. **Turnover Reconciliation**: Match total turnover reported in Audited Financial Statements with Table 5 & 7 of Form GSTR-9C, accounting for unbilled revenue, advances received, and stock transfers.
2. **Reverse Charge Mechanism (RCM)**: Ensure RCM tax payable on GTA, legal fees, and foreign imports is fully declared in Table 4G and paid via electronic cash ledger.
3. **Credit Notes Adjustment**: Ensure credit notes issued regarding previous year supplies are appropriately adjusted in Table 4/5 based on statutory cutoff dates (October 30 following end of FY).
4. **HSN Summary Reporting**: Mandatory HSN reporting at 6-digit level for taxpayers with turnover > ₹5 Crores.

---

## Conclusion

Filing GSTR-9/9C requires structural alignment between accounting ledgers, monthly portal filings, and audited financial statements. Timely reconciliation prevents late filing fees under Section 47.
