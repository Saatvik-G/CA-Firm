## Technical Overview

The Central Board of Direct Taxes (CBDT) has issued circular notifications rationalizing specific Tax Deducted at Source (TDS) rates under the Income Tax Act, 1961, aimed at reducing compliance friction for small taxpayers while enhancing digital audit trails.

---

## 1. Key Modifications in TDS Rates

- **Section 194-O (Payment by E-commerce Operators)**: TDS rate on gross sale amount of goods or services facilitated through e-commerce operators has been reduced from **1% to 0.1%**.
- **Section 194-IB (TDS on Rent by Individuals/HUF)**: The threshold for deducting tax on monthly rent remains ₹50,000 per month, with the deduction rate rationalized to **2%** (down from 5%).
- **Section 194M (Payments to Contractors/Professionals by Individuals/HUF)**: Duly applicable for payments exceeding ₹50 Lakhs in a financial year, with tax rate adjusted to **2%**.

## Action Items for Tax Deductors

1. Update ERP tax engines with revised rate tables effective from the enforcement date.
2. Verify PAN-Aadhaar linking status of payees before deducting TDS; non-linked PANs attract higher TDS rates under **Section 206AA** (minimum 20%).
3. Reconcile quarterly TRACES portal data against general ledger TDS expense provisions.
