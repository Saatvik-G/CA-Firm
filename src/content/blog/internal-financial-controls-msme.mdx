## Executive Summary

Under Section 134(5)(e) and Section 143(3)(i) of the **Companies Act, 2013**, corporate entities are required to establish, maintain, and certify **Internal Financial Controls (IFC)**. While statutory auditors of specified small companies are exempted from issuing a formal separate audit opinion on Internal Financial Controls over Financial Reporting (IFCoFR), the operational necessity for robust internal controls remains critical for MSMEs seeking working capital funding, fraud prevention, and operational scaling.

This article outlines a pragmatic internal control framework tailored for growing micro, small, and medium enterprises.

---

## 1. Core Dimensions of Internal Financial Controls

Internal Financial Controls encompass policies and procedures adopted by an enterprise to ensure:

1. **Orderly and Efficient Conduct of Business**: Adherence to management policies, Delegation of Authority (DoA) matrices, and operational workflows.
2. **Safeguarding of Assets**: Preventing unauthorized access, physical loss, or misappropriation of fixed assets, cash, and inventory.
3. **Prevention & Detection of Frauds and Errors**: Dual-authorization controls, independent reconciliation, and segregation of duties (SoD).
4. **Accuracy & Completeness of Accounting Records**: Ensuring timely monthly ledger close routines and audit trails.

## 3. Developing a Risk-Control Matrix (RCM)

A structured Risk-Control Matrix (RCM) identifies high-risk process steps, existing controls, testing procedures, and ownership.

### Sample RCM for Inventory Management:

| Sub-Process | Identified Risk | Control Activity | Frequency | Control Owner |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Material Receipt** | Receipt of substandard or unauthorized items | Physical inspection & Gate Entry GRN match against approved Purchase Order | Daily | Stores Manager |
| **Stock Count** | Inventory ledger discrepancy & unauthorized scrap | Quarterly physical stock audit with variance reconciliation | Quarterly | Plant Accounts Lead |
| **Material Issue** | Unrecorded consumption or theft | Issue against signed Requisition Note matching Bill of Materials (BOM) | Daily | Production Head |

## 4. Section 43B(h) MSME Creditor Control System

With the enforcement of Section 43B(h) of the Income Tax Act, internal controls over **Accounts Payable (AP)** have become critical to avoid disallowance of expenses:

- **Vendor Tagging**: Classify all registered vendors in accounting software as Micro, Small, Medium, or Non-MSME based on Udyam certificates.
- **Contract Agreement Filing**: Archiving written agreements establishing credit terms (15 days default, max 45 days).
- **Payment Alerts**: Setting automated 30-day alerts in ERP systems to ensure disbursements complete prior to statutory deadlines.
